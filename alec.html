Alec Shalowitz Richweb NEATrack Internship Thoughts:

What I Learned at Richweb:

  • I gained hands-on experience with networking equipment and configurations.
  • I learned how to configure and use routing protocols like OSPF, BGP, and static routes.
  • I set up networking tools such as VLANs, VNETs, DHCP, DNS, and static IPs.

What I Liked the Most and Least:

I liked that we were free to experiment and troubleshoot configuration issues. It’s a unique experience to have your own sandbox network to break and learn from.

As for what I liked the least: I wish we had more interaction with Richweb employees, like observing real tickets. I understand it’s hard to coordinate, though.

What I Think Could Be Improved:

Introducing more types of labs—like the Splunk lab I created—would be interesting. More options would help expose us to various tools and applications.

Would I Recommend the Program?

I would definitely recommend the program to other students. It’s a unique opportunity to learn and implement networks in a sandbox environment.
